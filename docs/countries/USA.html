"My influences, Salvador Dali, Grant Wood, Francis Bacon and Henri Matisse. Their weird, wonderful art makes me see the world in a different light every single day. That is why I spend my days searching the street for potential art, people are my influences, life is my influence. I've been like this since I can remember, I never used to concentrate in any other lesson apart from art in elementary school. My family disapproved of me doing art, "You won't be able to get a job in Art, noone hires an artist, you're income will never support your life" but that never stopped me in undertaking a degree in fine art. I practiced different elements of art until I graduated from university, I never applied to any jobs after university because I didn't want someone telling me what to draw and by when. I wanted to be my own individual, that is why I spend my days drawing art and figuring out ways to sell and showcase my art in gallerys. To date I've held 15 showcases and I've been mentioned in the local newspapr for my unique way of drawing. That to me is success."

(Katie Toll, Academy of Art University)

"My parents always put pressure on education from a young age. I excelled in the majority of subjects and received a scholarship to study a joint maths and science degree. The scholarship was one of my most greatest achievements and I could finally repay my parents for their hard-work. It was until the second year of university when I realised my real passion in life. I started volunteering at a hospital to help patients recover from operations or just lend a hand to the over-worked staff, this was when I realised the passion and drive that doctors had to help and save humans. I dropped out of university to undertake a medical degree and I have now been a doctor for 3 years and I plan on volunteering in unprivileged countries."

(Jake Young, University of California & Stanford University)
